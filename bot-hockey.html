Bot Hockey

The Bot Hockey project focuses on developing a suite of competitive robots designed for strategic gameplay, leveraging advanced engineering principles to dominate in robotic hockey competitions.

Overview

The Bot Hockey project focuses on developing a team of specialized robots for competitive robotic hockey matches. Currently, the Enforcer Bot has been completed, offering powerful opponent interaction and defense clearing capabilities. This project demonstrates a strategic approach to robotic engineering, with more bots in development to complete the team.

Problem Statement


Competitive robotics demands agile and powerful robots capable of performing specialized roles. While the Enforcer Bot is designed to dominate the defensive side of gameplay, the Goalie and Striker bots are still under development to form a full team for optimized gameplay performance.

Solutions


  • The completed Enforcer Bot is built with neodymium magnets and heavy-duty components to clear defenders and interact with opponents during matches. It brings balance between power and precision.
  • The Goalie and Striker bots are being designed to complement the Enforcer Bot, adding agility and shooting capabilities to form a well-rounded team.

Impact


  • Specialized Defense: The Enforcer Bot excels at blocking opponents, making it a key component of the team’s defense strategy.
  • Energy Optimization: Future bots will integrate speed and power modes for energy-efficient performance across the team.

  1. Research & Planning: We identified the roles needed for Bot Hockey and began with designing the Enforcer Bot, which would set the foundation for the team. Research involved:
    • Game Dynamics: Understanding the strategic needs of each role—defender, goalie, and striker.
    • Component Selection: Evaluating materials for durability, agility, and performance.
    • Regulatory Requirements: Ensuring all bots comply with competition standards.
  2. Enforcer Bot Development:
    • Design & CAD Modeling: Using CAD software, we created detailed models of the Enforcer Bot to ensure precision in its design, testing, and fabrication.


    • Prototyping & Testing: 3D models were prototyped for real-world testing, allowing us to refine the robot’s performance before finalizing its design.
    • Integration: Prepared the Enforcer Bot for teamwork with the forthcoming Goalie and Striker bots, ensuring compatibility between all robots’ systems.
  3. Future Development:
    • Goalie & Striker Bots: Plans include using solenoid shooters for high-speed shots and incorporating H-drive mechanisms for improved movement in future bots.
    • Testing & Iteration: Ongoing refinement of all bots to ensure cohesive teamwork when the full set is deployed in competitions.

Takeaways & Learnings



Specialized Roles

Designing a single robot for a specific role (Enforcer Bot) has provided critical insights into balancing power and performance.

Prototyping Challenges

The importance of iterative testing to refine both hardware and software, ensuring the robot’s behavior aligns with game requirements.

CAD Efficiency

Utilizing CAD modeling for accurate design and prototyping significantly improved the development process, resulting in faster iterations and precise functionality.

Software & Electrical Integration

The integration of software control systems with electrical hardware, including motors and sensors, was key to achieving the desired robot behavior and interaction.

Collaboration

The process of creating a cohesive team of robots has underscored the importance of synergy between different bots’ functionalities.
